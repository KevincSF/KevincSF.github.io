Missionaries:
...

  • Colonial Economic Disparities:(pg 122)

    • Mentioned previously, there are notable differences in the economic outcomes of former colonies.
      • Not limited to wealthier former British colonies (e.g., US, Canada, Australia, New Zealand).
      • Examples of middle-income countries with positive economic trajectories: Argentina, Brazil, Costa Rica, Botswana.
      • Conversely, some former colonies remain impoverished, such as Haiti, Mali, Sudan, and Niger.

  • Role of Missionaries in Economic Development: (pg 123)

    • A key factor influencing the economic divergence among colonized nations is the presence of missionaries during the colonial era.
      • Missionaries were sent by Christian churches as part of the colonization process to convert local populations.
      • Emphasizes that the positive outcomes are not directly linked to Christian values but rather to unintended consequences of missionary activities.

  • Woodberry's Findings on Democracy and Missionaries (Woodberry, 2012):(pg 123)

    • Woodberry's research suggests that the presence of Protestant missions explains a significant portion of democracy variation in Africa, Asia, Latin America, and Oceania.
      • Democracy's flourishing in regions with missionaries is attributed to factors like mass education, mass printing, newspapers, and civil society.
      • Protestant missionaries played a crucial role in promoting education, as evidenced by its positive association with missionary activity in India.

  • Education and Democracy Link (Lankina and Getachew, 2012):(pg 123)

    • Education is identified as a crucial aspect linked to democracy.
      • Highly literate societies with strong civil societies tend to support democracy.
      • Missionaries, primarily driven by conversion goals, unintentionally contributed to the promotion of education, particularly in the context of reading and interpreting the Bible.

  • Contemporary Educational Attainment and Missionaries:(pg 123)

    • The literature consistently shows that places where missionaries operated tend to have higher contemporary educational attainment.
      • In Africa, Cagé and Rueda (2016) find positive socio-economic outcomes near Protestant missions with printing presses.
      • In South America, Valencia Caicedo (2018) associates proximity to (Catholic) Jesuit missions with higher current education attainment.
      • Waldinger (2017) in Mexico and Bai and Kung (2015) in 19th-century China report similar positive effects on literacy rates and educational attainment due to mission activities.
